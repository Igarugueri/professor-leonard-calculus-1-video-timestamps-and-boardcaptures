в—Ҹ [32:10]. Applications in Business and Economics (Marginal Cost)
     в—ү Definition of Marginal Cost
          в—Ӣ Marginal cost refers to the additional cost incurred by producing one more unit of a good or service.
               в–  Economic Decision-Making: Businesses use marginal cost analysis to decide whether producing an additional unit will be profitable. 
                  If revenue from selling one more unit exceeds its marginal cost, it is beneficial to produce that unit.
                    в–Ј Before deciding to significantly increase production, companies analyze whether the extra revenue they will receive from selling more outweighs the increasingly higher marginal cost.
                           If the selling price is greater than the marginal cost, itвҖҷs worth producing more. If not, itвҖҷs better to stop there.
                    в–Ј **Production decisions are made based on the current level of output (вҖңShould I produce one more unit or not?вҖқ).**
                           ThatвҖҷs why marginal cost is useful for deciding whether itвҖҷs worth increasing production вҖ” and for that, you need to know how much the next unit at your current level will cost.
          в—Ӣ It is the rate of change of the total production cost
          в—Ӣ Marginal Cost is the first derivative of the total cost function рқ‘Ә(рқ’ҷ), where рқ’ҷ is the number of items produced: Marginal Cost = рқ‘Ә'(рқ’ҷ)
               в–  **Just as velocity is the rate at which position changes with respect to time, marginal cost is the rate at which total cost changes with respect to quantity.**
                    In essence, рқ‘ӘвҖІ(1) вүҲ рқ‘Ә(2) вҲ’ рқ‘Ә(1) captures the idea of how cost increases when producing one more unit from the current level вҖ” itвҖҷs not about the cost of the first unit itself,
                    but the _additional_ cost at the margin.

      • Time $рқ’•$ must be greater than or equal to zero $(рқ’•\ge 0)$ since you cannot go back in time

    • 02:45. Objectives of the Sales Model Analysis
      вһҖ Find the rate of change of sales
      вһҒ Find when sales will reach their peak
      вһӮ Find how quickly sales increase right when the movie is released (at $рқ’•=0$)
    • 04:40. вһҖ Calculating the Rate of Change (the derivative $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)$ or $\dfrac{dрқ‘ә}{dрқ’•}$)
      • The rate of change is the slope
      • Notation of the derivative with respect to $рқ’•$: $\dfrac{d}{dрқ’•}$
      • Using the Quotient Rule to derive $рқ‘ә(рқ’•)$
      • $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)=\dfrac{(рқ’•^{2}+1)\cdot \dfrac{d}{dрқ’•}[7рқ’•]-7рқ’•\cdot \dfrac{d}{dрқ’•}[рқ’•^{2}+1]}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}$
      • $=\dfrac{7(рқ’•^{2}+1)-7рқ’•\cdot 2рқ’•}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}$
      • $=\dfrac{7рқ’•^{2}+7-14рқ’•^{2}}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}=\dfrac{7-7рқ’•^{2}}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}$
      • Formula for the rate of change $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)=\dfrac{7-7рқ’•^{2}}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}$
      • Interpretation: it tells how fast sales are increasing or decreasing at any moment $рқ’•$
    • 09:10. вһҒ Finding when sales reach their peak
      • At a peak (maximum point), the slope (rate of change) is zero, since the function changes from increasing to decreasing
      • Set the derivative to zero to find where the slope is horizontal: $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)=0$
      • For a rational function to be zero, the numerator must be zero (the denominator cannot be zero or the function is undefined)
      • Set $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)=0$:
        • $\dfrac{7-7рқ’•^{2}}{(рқ’•^{2}+1)^{2}}=0$
        • $7-7рқ’•^{2}=0\;\;\rightarrow\;\;7=7рқ’•^{2}\;\;\rightarrow\;\;рқ’•^{2}=1$
        • Solutions: $рқ’•=+1,-1$
      • Validating the time: since the domain is $рқ’•\ge 0$, $рқ’•=-1$ is not valid; thus, $рқ’•=1$ is valid
      • Conclusion: Sales reach their peak exactly one year later (at $рқ’•=1$)
    • 13:50. вһӮ Finding the initial rate of increase of sales (at $рқ’•=0$)
      • The movie release moment is $рқ’•=0$
      • Evaluate the rate of change function (derivative $рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)$) at $рқ’•=0$: $рқ‘ә'(0)$
      • $рқ‘ә'(0)=\dfrac{7-7(0)^{2}}{(0^{2}+1)^{2}}=\dfrac{7}{1}=7$
      • Interpretation: Sales initially increase at a rate of $7$ (units, likely millions of DVDs/year) at launch

    • Relationship between Position $рқ‘ә(рқ’•)$, Velocity $рқ“Ҙ(рқ’•)$, Acceleration $рқ“җ(рқ’•)$, and Jerk
      • Velocity $рқ“Ҙ(рқ’•)$ is the first derivative of Position $рқ‘ә(рқ’•)$ with respect to time $рқ’•$: $рқ“Ҙ(рқ’•)=рқ‘ә'(рқ’•)$
        • Velocity is how your position is changing with respect to time
      • Acceleration $рқ“җ(рқ’•)$ is the derivative of Velocity $рқ“Ҙ(рқ’•)$, i.e., the second derivative of Position: $рқ“җ(рқ’•)=рқ“Ҙ'(рқ’•)=рқ‘ә''(рқ’•)$
        • Acceleration is how your velocity is changing with respect to time
      • Jerk is the derivative of Acceleration $рқ“җ(рқ’•)$, i.e., the third derivative of Position: $\text{Jerk}(рқ’•)=рқ“җ'(рқ’•)=рқ“Ҙ''(рқ’•)=рқ‘ә'''(рқ’•)$
        • Jerk describes how acceleration is changing with respect to time

  • 32:10. Applications in Business and Economics (Marginal Cost)
    • Definition of Marginal Cost
      • Marginal cost refers to the additional cost incurred by producing one more unit of a good or service.
        • Economic Decision-Making: Businesses use marginal cost analysis to decide whether producing an additional unit will be profitable. If revenue from selling one more unit exceeds its marginal cost, it is beneficial to produce that unit.
          • Before deciding to significantly increase production, companies analyze whether the extra revenue they will receive from selling more outweighs the increasingly higher marginal cost. If the selling price is greater than the marginal cost, itвҖҷs worth producing more. If not, itвҖҷs better to stop there.
          • Production decisions are made based on the current level of output (вҖңShould I produce one more unit or not?вҖқ). ThatвҖҷs why marginal cost is useful for deciding whether itвҖҷs worth increasing production вҖ” and for that, you need to know how much the next unit at your current level will cost.
      • It is the rate of change of the total production cost
      • Marginal Cost is the first derivative of the total cost function $рқ‘Ә(рқ’ҷ)$, where $рқ’ҷ$ is the number of items produced: Marginal Cost $=рқ‘Ә'(рқ’ҷ)$
        • Just as velocity is the rate at which position changes with respect to time, marginal cost is the rate at which total cost changes with respect to quantity. In essence, $рқ‘ӘвҖІ(1)\approx рқ‘Ә(2)-рқ‘Ә(1)$ captures the idea of how cost increases when producing one more unit from the current level вҖ” itвҖҷs not about the cost of the first unit itself, but the _additional_ cost at the margin.

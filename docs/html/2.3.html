  • 19:36. You need to understand the product rule first because it's one of the fundamental tools for differentiating expressions where two functions are multiplied: $\big(𝒇(𝒙)\cdot 𝓰(𝒙)\big)'=𝒇'(𝒙)\cdot 𝓰(𝒙)+𝒇(𝒙)\cdot 𝓰'(𝒙)$. Later on, you'll often encounter problems where this rule appears in combination with the chain rule: $\big(𝒇(𝓰(𝒙))\big)'=𝒇'\big(𝓰(𝒙)\big)\cdot 𝓰'(𝒙)$ or with the quotient rule: $\big(\dfrac{𝒇(𝒙)}{𝓰(𝒙)}\big)'=\dfrac{𝒇'(𝒙)\cdot 𝓰(𝒙)-𝒇(𝒙)\cdot 𝓰'(𝒙)}{\big(𝓰(𝒙)\big)^{2}}$. Understanding the product rule early will make it easier to handle these more complex derivative rules as they often work together.
